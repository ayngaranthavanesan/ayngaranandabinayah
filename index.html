FAQs
Everything you might be wondering
What should I wear?
Traditional attire is warmly encouraged — sarees, veshtis, and suits all look wonderful. Please avoid white, black, and red. Smart Western dress is also perfectly welcome.
Can I bring children?
Children are welcome at the traditional ceremony and reception. Please let us know on the RSVP form so we can ensure appropriate seating and food options.
Is there parking available?
Free parking available at the venues.
Are there dietary options?
Only vegetarian food will be served at the traditional Tamil wedding, and due to the large number of guests it is difficult for us to guarantee that all allergens can be catered for.
For those attending any of the other wedding events, please indicate your dietary options and allergens when you RSVP.
What is the thaali and why is the thaali time so important?
The thaali is a sacred gold necklace tied by the groom around the bride's neck at the most auspicious moment of the ceremony. This sacred time is determined through the alignment of the bride and groom's horoscopes with the positions of the stars and planets, and marks the moment we are officially wed in the eyes of our tradition — so please be seated well before this time!
Should I bring a gift?
Please do not bring boxed gifts to the venue — space is limited and we would hate for anything to be misplaced.
I have another question — who do I contact?
Please feel free to send any questions you have to ayngaranabinayah@gmail.com.
